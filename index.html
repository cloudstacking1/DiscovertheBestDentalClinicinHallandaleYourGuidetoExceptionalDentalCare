FAQ's

In-office teeth whitening is a professional treatment performed by a dentist, typically involving the application of a high-concentration bleaching gel activated by a special light. This method can achieve noticeable results in a single session, often lightening teeth by several shades. At-home teeth whitening involves using custom-fitted trays filled with a lower-concentration bleaching gel, which you wear over a period of time as directed by your dentist. While this method is more gradual, it can be effective and convenient for many patients.

Cosmetic dentistry encompasses various procedures aimed at enhancing the appearance of your teeth and smile. Treatments include: Veneers: Thin shells placed over the front of teeth to correct imperfections. Bonding: Application of tooth-colored resin to repair chips or gaps. Invisible Braces: Clear aligners used to straighten teeth discreetly.

Restorative dentistry focuses on repairing and replacing damaged or missing teeth to restore function and appearance. Common procedures include: Fillings: Used to treat cavities and restore tooth structure. Crowns: Caps placed over damaged teeth to restore shape and strength. Bridges: Devices used to replace one or more missing teeth by anchoring to adjacent teeth. Implants: Surgical placements that serve as a foundation for replacement teeth.
